Features

Automated queuing

Our bot helps you join a queue, tells you which jobs are needed to fill it, tells you when your game is ready to join and provides an easy way to report the results after.

Smart matchmaking

Our bot separates MMR per role and intelligently sets up teams based on the role you queue as.

Invite-only club

Being invite only we very specifically screen only the players whom we think will be able to contribute positively to our community. Players will be added on the basis that they can act maturely in disagreements and also have a fair understanding of the game

Club guidelines meant to protect players

We will be looking to actively keep our community toxicity free and drama free. This means that we will look to take action against anyone who would behave disruptively in our games or our Discord.

Personal stat tracking

Our bot will be able to provide your winrates and MMR as well as break them down for each job role you play.

Queue on demand

The bot will allow anyone to queue or leave at any time that fits their own schedule.

Alternate game modes

The bot will allow for players to join not just the normal mode, but an all random mode or a draft mode.

Trusted admins

Our admins are invested in the quality of the league and will help work with everyone to make the experience as enjoyable as possible.

Dedication to features

Our developers are constantly working on existing and future features in order to provide the most enjoyable experience possible with Feast PvP.

How it works

With the 4.1 cross-server custom match updates, players in the KIHC will be able to use bot commands in our Discord channel to queue with 7 other players. Once the queue is filled it will notify the players involved and they will start their own custom match with teams set by the bot.

The bot, using a proprietary MMR system, will match users according to our internal rankings to balance roles as evenly as possible. Once the match is complete, players will report the results to the bot and they’ll be able to enter into a new queue right after.

Bot Commands

  • User
  • Voucher
  • Admin
Join Queue
Example:!join [h, t, m, r]
Adds the player to the match queue for the datacenter of the channel they are executing this command from as the specified roles. The parameter after the letter ‘q’ specifies which singular role the user wishes to join the queue as.
'h' = Healer, 't' = Tank, 'm' = Melee, 'r' = Ranged
If no role is specified or more than one role is specified the user will be shown an error message and not entered into the queue. This command can be executed in multiple datacenter channels to add a player to multiple datacenter queues. Once the player has been entered into a match he/she is removed from all queues in other datacenters.
Additionally, players cannot re-join a new queue if they were previously in a match and they have not reported the outcome of the match.
Join as Spectator
Example:!joinspec
This command allows users to join the queue as a spectator (pending spectator feature for cross world custom matches).
Spectators are also required to confirm they are still able to join with the !ready check when the bot whispers them the match is ready. They will be provided the match password and instructed to join as a spectator only.
Ready for Match
Example:!ready
When the bot matches a game all 8 players are whispered by the bot to confirm they are ready and not AFK. If one or more do not confirm, they are removed from queue and given a temporary warning. Once 3 warnings occur they are suspended for 60 minutes with the !timeout command.
If one or more players do not confirm, the players who do !ready confirm are placed back at the top of the queue.
Leave Queue
Example:!leave
If a player has been in queue but something comes up and they are no longer able to participate in the next match the player uses this command to leave the queue. If the player is in multiple queues on different datacenters this will leave the player from all queues.
If admins detect an abuse of this command we will implement an autonomous system to warn/timeout users who leave too often in a designated timeframe.
Report Win
Example:!win
This command allows players who are currently existing in a match to report to the bot that their team has won the match.
Once 5/8 players report the match (i.e. 3 win, 2 lose or any combination of 5) the match is recorded as complete and MMR is awarded based on match outcome. If a quorum of 5/8 is not reached within 5 minutes of a match being reported by a player in the match and there are no conflicting reports the results that have been reported will be used to determine the match outcome.
If there are any conflicting reports, the match will not be recorded and admins will be notified.
Additionally, players cannot re-join a new queue if they were previously in a match and they have not reported the outcome of the match.
Report Lose
Example:!lose
(See above for details, same as !win command)
Show Queue
Example:!showq
This command allows any user to see which roles are missing for a match to begin.
Show User Role
Example:!role [user]
This command allows users to see the current role of a user, particularly if the user has the “voucher” role. Any user can see any other users roles.
Show Stats
Example:!stats
Whispers the user his/her personal statistical data, including their MMR and winrates for each role. Does not have any parameters and can only be used to fetch your own stats.
NOTE:
This means each player has a unique MMR per role and per datacenter.
Vouch
Example:!vouch [user]
This command allows any voucher to “vouch” for a user, where [user] is the new player’s discord handle/username.
If the user executing this command is an admin or super admin the player being vouched does not require a second vouch and is automatically added to vouched player pool.
For now, once a player has received two vouches from players with the “voucher” role he/she is added to the league but super admins have an option to enable an approval layer where admins must approve a player who has received the two required vouches first. For now this feature is disabled but can be enabled at a later time.
Set Role
Example:!setrole [role] [user]
This admin command allows admins to add a role to a user, for example “voucher” role.
Possible roles for admins to use: "voucher"
Possible roles for superadmins to use: "voucher", "admin"
User Data
Example:!data [user]
This admin command allows admins to view data about the specified player, such as how long they have been in the league, when they joined, who vouched them and their player stats.
Timeout or Suspend
Example:!timeout [mins] [user]
This admin command will temporarily suspend a user from queueing into a match for a specified timeframe in minutes.
Ban
Example:!ban [user]
This admin command will mark the specified user as “ban” and restrict the user from using any bot commands or queueing for a match.
NOTE: The user’s data will not be removed from the system since there is no direct benefit to this.
Clear Queue
Example:!clear
This admin command allows admins to completely remove all players currently in the match queue for the datacenter this command was executed from.
Remove From Queue
Example:!remove [user]
This admin command will remove the specified user from all queues across all datacenters.
Queue Info
Example:!queueinfo
An admin command which returns data about the current queue for the datacenter-channel the command was executed in.
Count Players
Example:!count
An admin command which returns the total number of players vouched for in the KIHC.
View Approvals
Example:!approvals
This admin command returns the current list of users who have been vouched but require admin approval before they are added to the league.
This command is only accessible when super admin command !vouchapproval is set to “on”.
Approve User
Example:!approve [user]
This admin command approves a user who has been vouched and is waiting for admin approval. The list of users requiring approval can be found using the !approvals command described above.
This command is only accessible when super admin command !vouchapproval is set to “on”.

Rules of Vouching / What it means to Vouch

When you vouch someone , you’re essentially introducing a new friend into your current group of friends. You’re telling everyone, “hey, guys, this is my new buddy, this person is cool I think they will fit in with everyone else.” Our hopes are the same as yours, and while there are no guarantees how people work with each other over the internet, here are our main points.
  • First, we very specifically are looking to add players who can handle wins, losses, and the other typical interactions of a competitive environment in a mature manner. Can a player keep a cool-head after a losing a close match where someone made a mistake? Are they able to be patient with other people when those guys might be learning new roles? That works for us and that’s what we want here. Or does a player flip out and start talking down to someone who didn’t understand any better, or harass them for their own peace of mind? That absolutely does not work for us.
  • Second, a player should have a reasonable expectation of how to play the game mode. As mentioned before, they don’t need to have thousands of hours of experience or any specific ranks or winrates, but they should know what they’re doing to a fair degree. We all want to have quality games and bringing in someone who is brand new would only make their gameplay experience far too difficult and likely unenjoyable. Everyone in the club wants to queue knowing they can count on their teammates to at least play decently.
  • Third, make entirely sure that player is who they say they are. Be familiar with them and what kind of person they are. Are they someone who will be fun for others to play with or will they be disruptive?
So take care in whom you vouch. We see vouching as a privilege that you earn but also one that can be rescinded if players aren’t careful with it. So please be responsible with your vouches.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is KIHC for? What level of experience do I need to play?

There is no set rank, skill level, or winrate requirement to get in, but players must be decent to each other and to be willing to learn. Players must also have a fundamental understanding of how the the Feast game mode works (more specifically how to handle medals).

I’m currently in another league. How will this affect me?

It won’t. Again, this club is about you being able to join a queue at any time you wish. There are no schedules. We might host a scheduled night where we run an all-random mode or a draft mode, but other than that you queue at your own leisure.

I’m worried about people being abusive either through chat or voice.

That’s not what this club’s about or what we are going to allow. So if there’s an incident we halt that person’s queuing privileges the first time. The second time we handle things internally. Ideally what we want are for players to be able to act like functional adults and to police themselves when issues arise. Other than that our emphasis will be to protect our players. There is not going to be any berating of someone who isn’t as good as you. There is not going to be hostility to someone who lost you a game. There is not going to be talking down to others because they made mistakes. You engage and speak like adults or you move on. If you can’t act like one when things don’t go your way then the club isn’t for you. Abuse of any sort does not belong here.

I want to vouch someone or a friend wants me to vouch for them. What are the requirements for vouching?

The main thing we ask of members is that you vouch people whom you know will be able to contribute positively to the club. That mostly means someone who won’t be toxic towards each other in and out of games, someone who has some viable experience and understands how Feast works on a fundamental level, and someone who is willing to work with others to help sustain positive gameplay experiences. But if you really wanna boil it down, basically you wanna vouch people who will be good to each other and who can do coin math (seriously don’t be vouching people who think 4 stacking is ok). They shouldn’t be brand new to pvp or Feast, but invites shouldn’t have to have thousands of games just to join.

When does KIHC start?

The club will start once cross server custom parties go live in 4.1. We have not determined how long seasons will go.

Anything else you plan to add?

In the future we plan to add an all random mode where players join the queue and are assigned random roles. Additionally we plan to add a draft feature where each team goes back and forth on picking jobs out of the overall pool until the teams are set. Otherwise, we will be working on new features constantly and we welcome any suggestions!

How do I apply? What else do I need to get in?

As we are an invite-only club, any new applicants from Oct 10th forward will require two vouches from established members. Once an applicant gets those vouches they simply require group approval from the admins to make sure they aren’t on any excluded lists and they will be added to the roster. You do need Discord, at the very least. We will not require members to use a mic but we will encourage players to at least listen in during a match.

I have some difficult history with someone else who is in the club. How will this affect me?

All we ask is that you try and work together when you play with each other, and to not antagonize each other when you’re out of the game. We’re not asking anyone to be best friends with someone you don’t want to be best friends with. We just want you to be able to play nice and act maturely with others. Everyone invited thus far is here under the pretense we can leave past issues at the door. Being drama free is a top priority for us.

I vouched for someone and they ended up misrepresenting who they were or they were banned from the club. What happens now?

The whole point of the voucher system is that you take responsibility for that player entering into the club. The person you vouch for represents your stake, as you yourself represent what the club is about. Of course you can’t control what someone says or does and it’s not your role to do that. So you vouch only when you’re sure that the person will be a good addition to our community. You vouch when you know that person is exactly who they say they are. Not everything works out all the time so if someone flames out then the persons vouching for them will have their vouching privileges restricted for a time.

Which datacenters is KIHC available on?

We are initially running KIHC on Primal and Aether DC’s. If we find our model to work well and there is interest on other DC’s we will expand wherever we have enough people.

Will KIHC have leaderboards?

Currently we are going to make leaderboards invisible. We will allow players to use bot commands that lets them privately see their winrates, MMR, and MMR per role. We might potentially release partial leaderboards at the end of each season but for the moment we just want the bot to accurately handle matchmaking. We want people to focus on playing and enjoying themselves whether it’s to get better on their favorite jobs or to try out new ones.

Can I watch games?

We plan to add bot commands that lets members join a queue as a spectator.

Story behind the Inhouse Club

Some time ago, before Feast PvP existed, a small but dedicated group of us from Primal would try and queue nightly for Wolves’ Den PvP. We had players from Behemoth, Ultros, Leviathan, Excalibur, and several other servers all working together just to get games rolling on demand, and our nights would last for hours. As time went on it became more challenging to hold PvP nights, though the passion remained the same for many players. Since then, many players, including myself have been looking for ways to play quality games with respectable players the same way we did in the past.

With 4.1 here and the option for cross-server custom matches a now available feature we feel we can realize some of those ideals. With a bot that can automate intelligent matchmaking and a playerbase that cares about having those quality games, we feel that we can create a platform that encapsulates all of that. And that’s what KIHC is for.
