Frequently Asked Questions

Who is KIHC for? What level of experience do I need to play?

There is no set rank, skill level, or winrate requirement to get in, but players must be decent to each other and to be willing to learn. Players must also have a fundamental understanding of how the the Feast game mode works (more specifically how to handle medals).

I want to vouch someone or a friend wants me to vouch for them. What are the requirements for vouching?

The main thing we ask of members is you vouch people whom you know will be able to contribute positively to the club. That mostly mean someone who won’t be toxic toward each other in and out of games, someone who has some viable experience and understands how Feast works on a fundamental level, and someone who is willing to work with others to help sustain positive gameplay experiences. But if you really wanna boil it down, basically you wanna vouch people who will be good to each other and who can do coin math (seriously don’t be vouching people who think 4 stacking is ok). They shouldn’t be brand new to pvp or Feast, but invites shouldn’t have to have thousands of games just to join.

Oops, I lost my cool on someone. What happens now?

That’s not what this club’s about, so if there’s an incident and you go off on someone, we halt your queuing privileges the first time. The second time we handle things internally. Ideally what we want are for players to be able to act like functional adults and police themselves when issues arise, whether you are an adult or not. If you can’t act like one when things don’t go your way then the club isn’t for you. There is not going to be any berating of someone who isn’t as good as you. There is not going to be being hostile to someone who lost you a game. There is not going to be talking down to others because they made mistakes. You engage and talk like adults or you move on. We will protect our players. It’s pretty simple: don’t be a jerk to someone else.

When does KIHC start?

The club will start once cross server custom parties go live in 4.1. We have not determined how long seasons will go.

How do I apply? What else do I need to get in?

As we are an invite-only club, any new applicants from Oct 10th and forward will require two vouches from established members. Once an applicant gets those vouches they simply require group approval from the admins to make sure they aren’t on any excluded lists and they will be added to the roster. You do need Discord, at the very least. We will not require members to use a mic but we will encourage players to at least listen in during a match.

I have some difficult history with someone else who is in the club. How will this affect me?

All we ask is that you try and work together when you play with each other, and not antagonize each other when you’re out of the game. We’re not asking anyone to be best friends with someone you don’t want to be best friends with. We just want you to be able to play nice and act maturely with others. Starting drama is not in our MO.

I vouched for someone and they ended up misrepresenting who they were or they were banned from the club. What happens now?

The whole point of the voucher system is that you take responsibility for that player entering into the club. The person you vouch for represents your stake, as you yourself represent what the club is about. Of course you can’t control what someone says or does and it’s not your role to do that. So you vouch only when you’re sure that the person will be a good addition to our community. You vouch when you know that person is exactly who they say they are. And if that person flames out and causes issues we won’t say that it’s all on you, but we will restrict your vouching privileges for a time.

Which datacenters is KIHC available on?

We are initially running KIHC on Primal and Aether DC’s. If we find our model to work well and there is interest on other DC’s we will expand wherever we have enough people.

Will KIHC have leaderboards?

Currently we are going to make leaderboards invisible. We will allow players to use bot commands that lets them privately see their winrates, MMR, and MMR per role. We might potentially release partial leaderboards at the end of each season but for the moment we just want the bot to accurately handle matchmaking. We want people to focus on playing and enjoying themselves whether it’s to get better on their favorite jobs or to try out new ones.
